SCENARIO CONTEXT: Low IV + Bullish (IV rank <40%, price > EMA20 > EMA50)

IV is cheap and price is trending up — buying premium is favorable.
Options are underpriced relative to history; buy directional exposure with a defined ceiling.

PRIMARY STRATEGY: Bull Call Spread or Long Call

Reasoning:
- Low IV means buying premium is cheap — ideal for debit spreads
- Uptrend gives directional edge
- Spread limits cost while maintaining upside exposure

Execution rules:
- Primary (max convexity): Long ATM or slightly-ITM CE outright when the uptrend is
  strong and the premium fits the 7% risk cap. A long call's max loss is the premium
  paid — this is the highest-upside, defined-risk growth trade in the playbook.
- Alternative (cheaper, capped upside): Bull Call Spread — buy ATM CE, sell OTM CE at
  the nearest resistance / OI wall — only when an outright call exceeds the 7% cap.

Sizing rule: Max loss = net debit x lot size x lots. This must be ≤ the
"Max risk this trade (7%)" figure shown in MARKET CONTEXT. Size lots from that live
number, never a fixed rupee figure.

Profit management (let the winner run — this is where account growth comes from):
- Book 50% of the position at +100% of premium paid.
- Move the stop on the remainder to breakeven, then trail it at +50% of running profit.
- Do NOT close the whole position at the first target. A runaway trend out of a
  low-IV base is exactly the asymmetric payoff this account is built to capture.

Key risk: Theta burn if the move doesn't happen within DTE/2.
Time stop: if spot hasn't moved in your favour by the halfway point to expiry, exit
regardless of P&L and recycle the capital into the next setup.
