SCENARIO CONTEXT: Low IV + Bullish (IV rank <40%, price > EMA20 > EMA50)

IV is cheap and price is trending up — buying premium is favorable.
Options are underpriced relative to history; buy directional exposure with a defined ceiling.

PRIMARY STRATEGY: Bull Call Spread or Long Call

Reasoning:
- Low IV means buying premium is cheap — ideal for debit spreads
- Uptrend gives directional edge
- Spread limits cost while maintaining upside exposure

Execution rules:
- Buy ATM CE (at-the-money call)
- Sell OTM CE at the nearest resistance level (OI wall or technical resistance)
- Total premium paid must be under ₹3,000 across all lots
- If a single ATM call fits within ₹3,000 budget, a long call is acceptable

Key risk: Theta burn if the move doesn't happen within DTE/2.
If spot hasn't moved in your favor by the halfway mark to expiry, reassess and exit.

Sizing rule: Total premium paid (net debit x lot size x number of lots) must be under ₹3,000.
This is your maximum loss on the trade. Size lots accordingly.

Time awareness: With low IV and a directional bet, time is your enemy.
Set a time stop at DTE/2 — if the trade isn't working by then, exit regardless of P&L.
