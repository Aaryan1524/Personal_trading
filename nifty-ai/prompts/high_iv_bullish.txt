SCENARIO CONTEXT: High IV + Bullish (IV rank >60%, price > EMA20 > EMA50)

IV is elevated but price is above both EMAs — trend is up, fear is high.
Sell puts in the direction of the trend. The trend is your tailwind; IV is your edge.

PRIMARY STRATEGY: Bull Put Spread

Reasoning:
- Elevated IV makes put selling lucrative
- Uptrend means spot is moving away from the short put
- Defined risk keeps max loss capped in case of sudden reversal

Execution rules:
- Sell ATM-1 PE (one strike below ATM)
- Buy ATM-3 PE for protection (three strikes below ATM)
- Spread width: keep under 200 points for BankNifty, under 100 points for Nifty
- Net credit should be at least 30% of the spread width

Key risk: Sudden reversal or gap-down on news.
If spot breaks below EMA20 intraday, treat as invalidation and exit.

Sizing rule: Keep spread width under ₹200 (BankNifty) or ₹100 (Nifty).
This keeps max loss per lot within risk tolerance.

Alternative if trend is very strong: Sell a single OTM put (cash-secured) instead of a spread,
but only if defined capital can absorb the margin and max loss stays under ₹4,000.
