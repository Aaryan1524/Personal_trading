SCENARIO CONTEXT: Neutral / Range-bound (IV rank 40-60%, price between S1 and R1)

IV is in the middle range and price is consolidating between support and resistance.
No strong directional bias — this is an Iron Condor environment.

PRIMARY STRATEGY: Iron Condor

Reasoning:
- Mid-range IV and sideways price action favor theta decay plays
- Iron Condor collects premium from both sides with defined risk
- S1 and R1 define the natural wing placement

Execution rules:
- Sell CE at R1 (resistance level)
- Buy CE at R1 + 200 (Nifty) or R1 + 500 (BankNifty) for protection
- Sell PE at S1 (support level)
- Buy PE at S1 - 200 (Nifty) or S1 - 500 (BankNifty) for protection
- Verify: total net premium collected per lot must exceed ₹80/lot minimum
  If it doesn't, the trade is not worth taking — widen the spread or skip

Key risk: A breakout in either direction beyond the short strikes.
Set a hard stop if spot closes beyond either short strike.

Sizing check: compute exact premium collected before entering.
Net credit = (CE premium sold - CE premium bought) + (PE premium sold - PE premium bought)
If net credit x lot size x lots < ₹1,000 total, skip the trade — not enough reward.

Adjustment rule: if one side gets tested, roll the untested side closer to collect more premium
and bring the position delta back to neutral.

Capital check (mandatory before outputting legs):
Compute: max loss = (wing width in points - net credit collected) x lot size x lots.
Max loss must be ≤ ₹4,000. If the 200pt/500pt wings produce max loss > ₹4,000,
narrow the wings or reduce lots until max loss fits. Show your calculation explicitly.
