SCENARIO CONTEXT: Low IV + Bearish (IV rank <40%, price < EMA20 < EMA50)

IV is cheap and price is trending down — buy puts in the direction of the trend.
Downside momentum with cheap options is a good setup for a bear put spread.

PRIMARY STRATEGY: Bear Put Spread

Reasoning:
- Low IV means put buying is cheap relative to history
- Downtrend gives directional edge for put buyers
- Spread caps cost and defines maximum loss

Execution rules:
- Buy ATM PE (at-the-money put)
- Sell OTM PE at the nearest support level (OI wall or technical support)
- Total premium paid must be under ₹3,000 across all lots
- Spread width should reflect the distance to the next support zone

Key risk: Mean reversion rally. If spot breaks above EMA20, the bearish thesis is broken.
Exit immediately if EMA20 is reclaimed on a closing basis.

Sizing rule: Total net debit (premium paid x lot size x lots) must stay under ₹3,000.
This is the same constraint as low_iv_bullish — buying premium always has this cap.

Time awareness: Set a time stop at DTE/2.
If the market hasn't moved lower by then, theta will erode the remaining value — exit.

Watch the PCR: if it starts rising sharply (put writing increasing), it may signal a reversal.
