SCENARIO CONTEXT: Low IV + Bearish (IV rank <40%, price < EMA20 < EMA50)

IV is cheap and price is trending down — buy puts in the direction of the trend.
Downside momentum with cheap options is a good setup for a bear put spread.

PRIMARY STRATEGY: Bear Put Spread

Reasoning:
- Low IV means put buying is cheap relative to history
- Downtrend gives directional edge for put buyers
- Spread caps cost and defines maximum loss

Execution rules:
- Primary (max convexity): Long ATM or slightly-ITM PE outright when the downtrend is
  strong and the premium fits the 7% risk cap. A long put's max loss is the premium
  paid — highest-upside, defined-risk growth trade for a falling market.
- Alternative (cheaper, capped upside): Bear Put Spread — buy ATM PE, sell OTM PE at
  the nearest support / OI wall — only when an outright put exceeds the 7% cap.

Sizing rule: Max loss = net debit x lot size x lots. This must be ≤ the
"Max risk this trade (7%)" figure shown in MARKET CONTEXT. Size lots from that live
number, never a fixed rupee figure.

Profit management (let the winner run — this is where account growth comes from):
- Book 50% of the position at +100% of premium paid.
- Move the stop on the remainder to breakeven, then trail it at +50% of running profit.
- Do NOT close the whole position at the first target. A fast breakdown is the
  asymmetric payoff this account is built to capture.

Key risk: Mean reversion rally. If spot reclaims EMA20 on a closing basis, the bearish
thesis is broken — exit immediately.
Time stop: if the market hasn't moved lower by the halfway point to expiry, exit
regardless of P&L — theta will erode the remaining value.

Watch the PCR: if it starts rising sharply (put writing increasing), it may signal a reversal.
